Understanding Utah Women's Financial Perceptions

Data insights from the "A Bolder Way Forward" research initiative

How do Utah County women's perceptions towards finance compare to Utahns' perceptions overall?

When examining financial perceptions across Utah, a remarkable pattern emerges. Utah County women consistently demonstrate higher confidence, awareness, and engagement with financial topics than the state average. But just how significant is this difference?

Let's start with the foundation: the importance of financial competence for women.

The Real Difference: This stunning 14-point gap reveals the true advantage of Utah County women. While recognition of financial importance is universal, Utah County women are significantly more likely to take action and actually use available financial resources.

Strategic Implications & Recommendations

Leverage High-Performing Communities

The success of LDS women in Utah County suggests that concentrated community support, educational resources, and cultural emphasis on financial responsibility create measurable outcomes. Understanding and replicating these community-level factors could benefit women statewide.

Bridge the Awareness-to-Action Gap

While financial literacy awareness is relatively strong, converting that awareness into resource utilization represents the greatest opportunity for impact. Targeted interventions should focus on removing barriers to resource access and creating more accessible pathways to financial tools and education.

Expand Geographic Access

The consistent Utah County advantage across all metrics suggests that geographic proximity to resources matters significantly. Expanding access to financial resources and education beyond central population centers could help close the statewide gaps in financial empowerment.

Bottom Line: Utah has a strong foundation of women who value financial competence, but significant opportunities exist to translate that recognition into resource utilization and practical financial confidence, particularly outside of Utah County's concentrated support ecosystem.
